Want to make your own?
Here are some tips I use

Start on a random page. Don't feel like you have to go in order. It helps me a lot because I don't get that "halfway through" stress, so it just fills up naturally whenever I feel like adding to it.

Stop trying to make it perfect. It's a junk journal, not a pretty one! Every page is unique because it's yours, so don't worry if it looks messy. You are doing this for you, not for an art gallery.

Choose your own vibe. You don't have to pick just one type of journal. I have my Lidl Book for work stuff but you could do bus tickets, travel memories, or even candy wrappers. Whatever you are into just go for it.

Don't force your workflow. Some weeks I journal every day, and other weeks I don't touch it for ages and that's totally okay. It shouldn't ever feel like a chore.

Keep your tools simple. You don't need expensive supplies, just a pen, scissors, and glue. Pro tip get some double sided tape for things like chip bags or shiny packaging, because regular glue usually peels off after a few days.

Don't feel obligated to mix text and images. Some days I just write like it's a diary, and other days I just collage with stickers or receipts. Just do whatever matches your mood that day.

Most importantly, just have fun with it. It doesn't need to look professional it just needs to be made by you. Stick, scribble, and be yourself.
